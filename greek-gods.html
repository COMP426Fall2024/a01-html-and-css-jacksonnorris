Greek Gods

The 12 major Greek gods, also known as the Olympians due to their residence on Mount Olympus, are Zeus, Poseidon, Hera, Demeter, Aphrodite, Athena, Artemis, Apollo, Ares, Hephaestus, Hermes, and Dionysus. However, this is not a definitive list, as some lists will replace Dionysus with Hestia. There are not 13 Olypmians most likely due to the significance of the number 12 in ancient Greek society.
