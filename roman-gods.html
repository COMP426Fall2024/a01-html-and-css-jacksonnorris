Roman Gods

The most known Roman gods are actually the ones that serve as counterparts to the Greek Olympians. Jupiter, the ruler of Mount Olympus, is the Roman equivalent to Zeus. Jupiter's wife is Juno, equivalent to Hera in Greek mythology. The remaining 10 are Neptune (Greek: Poseidon), Mars (Ares), Minerva (Athena), Ceres (Demeter), Apollo, Diana (Artemis), Venus (Aphrodite), Vulcan (Hephaestus), Mercury (Hermes), and Bacchus (Dionysus). Apollo is the only one in the pantheon who maintains the same name in both traditions.
